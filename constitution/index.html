Structure

True religion is a matter of the heart, and the force of true religion dissipates as it is held by larger and larger entities. Christian Minimalism (XMin) is a denomination of the Christian religion that is minimally defined at the global level. XMin is not incorporated legally. It holds no property, has no clergy, and conducts no ecumenical relations. The household is the largest constituent entity that it recognizes, and it has no official records of membership. XMin defines no explicit doctrine; its official teaching is comprised entirely of this Constitution.

Christian Minimalism is organized as a voluntary association of individuals and households. An XMin individual or adherent of XMin is an adult who has been baptized in the name of the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit, and adheres to Christian Minimalism as defined in this Constitution. An XMin household is either a family in which both husband and wife are adherents of XMin, or a group of XMin individuals (celibate and of the same gender) who live together as an XMin household. A family is a husband and wife and their dependent children. XMin individuals who do not live in a household, including single parents and those married to a non-adherent of XMin, maintain a special relationship of affiliation with exactly one XMin household.

This Constitution is published on the World Wide Web at http://xmin.org/. Such minimal governance as XMin requires for the maintenance and publication of this Constitution is carried out via the Internet by a group of XMin individuals. Membership in this group is voluntary, self-selecting, and open to all adherents of XMin. All of the group's business is conducted transparently, in full public view. This Constitution is dedicated to the public domain.

Mission

The mission of Christian Minimalism is to convert the world to the Gospel of Jesus Christ.

Rule of Life

All adherents of XMin make the following four commitments, to:

  1. Worship
  2. Faithfulness
  3. Problem Solving
  4. Conversion

1. Worship

Worship is an act of corporate intimacy with God. It capitulates the general fellowship with God that is available to all people at all times. XMin prescribes a simple liturgy to be used by its adherents.

2. Faithfulness

Adherents of XMin remain faithful to household, neighborhood, and work relationships, in that order of priority. Relationships grow with time spent together, and time spent together in the real world is more valuable than time spent communicating otherwise.

3. Problem Solving

Problem solving in XMin is an approach to daily life. In solving problems from the personal to the international, adherents of XMin emphasize creativity, collaboration, optimism, non-violence, and a lack of judgmentalism.

4. Conversion

Adherents of XMin are responsible first for their own conversion and that of their household. The practices of Faithfulness and Problem Solving involve XMin individuals in their neighborhoods and workplaces, and in the world at large. As relationships allow, XMin households invite other families and individuals to participate in their practice of Worship, and in time perhaps to become adherents of XMin themselves.

Liturgy

The XMin liturgy has three services: Communion with God, Baptism, and Marriage. All passages of Scripture used in the liturgy are taken from the World English Bible. Service sheets for each liturgy are published on the XMin website.

Communion with God

On Sunday, the household gathers together with any guests around the dinner table before the mid-day meal to celebrate Communion with God. Before the service, good wine and good unleavened bread are set out, along with any service sheets needed.

The service begins with a recitation from Scripture, all joining together. The passage recited depends on the liturgical season, as follows:

Season Passage
Advent Deuteronomy 6:4-9
Christmas Psalm 23
Epiphany ???
Lent Exodus 20:1-17
Easter Philippians 2:5-11
Pentecost

After the recitation, those present share matters for prayer with one another. All are encouraged to speak, adherent or no. When all matters for prayer have been shared, the service continues with one of the three historic Creeds of Christianity: the Apostles', the Nicene (without the filioque), or the Athanasian. The Apostles' Creed is used during Lent, the Athanasian during Epiphany, and the Nicene at all other times. A time of open prayer follows the creed, concluding with the Lord's Prayer, said together:

Our Father, who art in heaven,
Hallowed be thy Name.
Thy kingdom come.
Thy will be done,
On earth as it is in heaven.
Give us this day our daily bread.
And forgive us our trespasses,
As we forgive those who trespass against us.
And lead us not into temptation,
But deliver us from evil.
For thine is the kingdom,
and the power, and the glory,
for ever and ever.
Amen.

Following the Lord's Prayer, the service continues with a second recitation from Scripture, all joining together. The passages used are as follows:

Season Passage
Advent Deuteronomy 6:4-9
Christmas Psalm 23
Epiphany ???
Lent Exodus 20:1-17
Easter Philippians 2:5-11
Pentecost

After the second recitation, the bread and wine are passed around the room in silence. Each XMin adherent eats a bite of the bread and drinks a sip of the wine. Children of XMin adherents age 16 or younger also receive. The service then concludes with a minute or two of further silence.

It is appropriate to accompany this service of Communion with God with a meal, music-making, Bible study, or other forms of fellowship.
